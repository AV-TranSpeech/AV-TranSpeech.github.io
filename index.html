AV-TranSpeech: Audio-Visual Robust Speech-to-Speech Translation

-->

Abstract

Direct speech-to-speech translation (S2ST) aims to convert speech from one language into another, and has demonstrated significant progress to date. Despite the recent success, current S2ST models still suffer from distinct degradation in noisy scenarios and fail to translate visual speech\footnote{We use visual speech to denote the movement of lips and teeth.}. In this work, we present AV-TranSpeech, the first audio-visual speech-to-speech (AV-S2ST) translation model without relying on intermediate text. AV-TranSpeech complements the audio stream with visual information to promote system robustness, opening up a host of practical applications: dictation or dubbing archival films. To mitigate the data scarcity with little parallel AV-S2ST data, we 1) explore self-supervised pre-training with unlabeled audio-visual data to learn contextual representation, and 2) introduce cross-modal distillation with S2ST models trained on the audio-only corpus to boost the performance of visual systems. Experimental results on two language pairs demonstrate that AV-TranSpeech outperform audio-only S2ST models under all settings regardless of the type of noise.
